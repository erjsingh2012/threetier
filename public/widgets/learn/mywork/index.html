📘 About Fort St. John

Fort St. John is the most populous city in British Columbia’s Peace River Regional District, covering approximately 32.7 km² with a population of 21,465 as of the 2021 Canadian Census.

Founded in 1794 as Rocky Mountain Fort, it is the oldest European‑established settlement on mainland British Columbia. The current city site has existed since 1925.

Located at an elevation of about 690 m, the city lies near the Peace and Beatton Rivers along the Alaska Highway (Mile 47). Fort St. John is a key regional hub for the energy, agriculture, and forestry industries and is known as “The Energetic City.”

The Pomeroy Sport Centre, opened in 2010, features an indoor long-track speed skating oval, one of only three in Canada.

Nearby attractions include Beatton Provincial Park, offering trails, lakefront access, and winter recreation on Charlie Lake.
